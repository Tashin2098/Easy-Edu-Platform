For Classes 9 to 10

Home

Mathematics

Trigonometry:

Introducing trigonometric functions (sine, cosine, tangent) and their applications in right triangles.

Probability and Statistics::

Understanding basic probability concepts such as outcomes, events, sample spaces, and probability rules.

Data and Statistics:

Calculating measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and dispersion (range, standard deviation).

Physics

Optics:

Understanding the laws of reflection and refraction and their applications in mirrors and lenses.

Sound:

Understanding the properties of sound waves, including frequency, wavelength, amplitude, and speed.

Modern Physics:

Introducing the structure of the atom, subatomic particles, and nuclear reactions.

Chemistry

Atomic Structure:

Exploring different atomic models proposed by scientists such as Dalton, Thomson, Rutherford, and Bohr.

Acids, Bases, and Salts:

Introducing the pH scale as a measure of acidity or basicity of a solution and its significance in everyday life.

Periodic Table:

Understanding the arrangement of elements in the periodic table based on atomic number, electronic configuration, and chemical properties.

Biology

Cell Biology:

Learning about cellular processes such as cell division (mitosis and meiosis), cellular respiration, photosynthesis, and protein synthesis.

Genetics:

Learning about the structure and function of DNA and RNA molecules, including DNA replication, transcription, and translation.

Microbiology:

Introducing different types of microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, protists, and their roles in ecosystems and human health.
