For Classes 4 to 8

Home

Mathematics

Numbers and Counting:

Learning to count forwards and backwards.

Geometry:

Introducing concepts like volume, surface area, and the Pythagorean theorem.

Algebra:

Solving equations and inequalities with variables.

Mathematical Reasoning and Problem-Solving:

Introducing logical reasoning, deductive thinking, and mathematical proofs.

Science

Physical Sciences:

Learning about the properties and states of matter (solid, liquid, gas) and the changes they undergo (melting, freezing, evaporation, condensation).

Earth and Space:

Earth's Structure and Processes.

Science and Society:

Exploring the role of science in society and its impact on technology, medicine, and the environment.

Life Sciences:

Genetics and Heredity.

English

Reading Comprehension:

Introducing students to a variety of literary genres such as fiction, nonfiction, poetry, drama, and informational texts.

Grammar and Punctuation:

Learning basic grammar concepts such as nouns, verbs, adjectives, and adverbs.

Writing Skills:

Teaching different types of writing such as narratives, descriptive essays, persuasive essays, expository essays, and creative writing.

History and Geography

Ancient Civilizations:

Introducing students to ancient civilizations such as Mesopotamia, Egypt, Greece, and Rome.

Revolutionary Movements:

Learning about key revolutionary movements such as the American Revolution, French Revolution, and Industrial Revolution.

Physical Geography:

Introducing students to basic concepts of physical geography, including landforms, bodies of water, climate zones, and natural resources.
